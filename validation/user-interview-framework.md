# User Interview Framework: JTBD-Based Validation

## Overview

This framework provides structured interview protocols for conducting minimally 10 user interviews per persona segment, with prompts based on journey mapping and Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) theory. Each interview synthesizes jobs, pains, and gains to validate and refine our understanding of user needs.

## Interview Objectives

### Primary Objectives
1. **Validate Personas:** Confirm persona characteristics and behaviors
2. **Discover Jobs:** Identify functional, emotional, and social jobs
3. **Uncover Pains:** Understand current pain points and frustrations
4. **Identify Gains:** Discover desired outcomes and aspirations
5. **Test Hypotheses:** Validate or invalidate product hypotheses
6. **Map Journeys:** Understand user journeys and touchpoints

### Secondary Objectives
1. **Willingness to Pay:** Gauge pricing sensitivity
2. **Feature Prioritization:** Understand what matters most
3. **Competitive Context:** Learn about current solutions
4. **Language Discovery:** Capture user language and terminology

## Interview Structure

### Pre-Interview Setup

**Recruitment Criteria (Per Persona):**
- **Solo Podcaster:** 1K-50K monthly downloads, 6+ months podcasting, has sponsorships
- **Producer:** Manages 5+ shows, 3+ years experience, handles sponsor relationships
- **Agency:** 10+ podcast clients, 2+ years in business, provides analytics services
- **Brand:** Spends $10K+ quarterly on podcast sponsorships, 2+ campaigns/year
- **Data Marketer:** Works with podcast attribution data, technical background
- **Sponsor:** Direct advertiser, $50K+ annual podcast budget

**Recruitment Channels:**
- LinkedIn outreach
- Podcast creator communities (Reddit, Discord, Facebook)
- Cold email to podcasters
- Agency partnerships
- Referrals from initial interviews

**Incentive Structure:**
- $50-100 gift card or equivalent
- Early access to product
- Free premium subscription (if applicable)
- Donation to their podcast

### Interview Format

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Format:** Video call (Zoom/Google Meet) with screen sharing capability
**Recording:** Audio + video (with consent)
**Note-taking:** Real-time notes + transcription

## Interview Protocol by Persona

### 1. Solo Podcaster Interview Protocol

#### Opening (5 minutes)
- **Warm-up:** "Tell me about your podcast - how long have you been doing it?"
- **Context Setting:** "I'm researching how podcasters manage sponsorships and analytics"
- **Consent:** Confirm recording and usage

#### Journey Mapping (15 minutes)
**Prompt 1: Onboarding Journey**
- "Walk me through how you first started tracking sponsorships. What tools did you use?"
- "What was the hardest part about getting started?"
- "If you could change one thing about that experience, what would it be?"

**Prompt 2: Campaign Launch Journey**
- "Describe the last time you set up a new sponsor campaign. What steps did you take?"
- "What took the longest? What was frustrating?"
- "What would make this process easier?"

**Prompt 3: Report Generation Journey**
- "Tell me about the last time you created a report for a sponsor. How long did it take?"
- "What information did you include? How did you gather it?"
- "How did the sponsor react? What questions did they ask?"

#### JTBD Deep Dive (15 minutes)
**Functional Jobs:**
- "When you think about managing sponsorships, what are you really trying to accomplish?"
- "What does success look like for you?"
- "What would make your life easier?"

**Emotional Jobs:**
- "How do you want to feel when working with sponsors?"
- "What emotions do you experience now? What would you prefer?"
- "What makes you feel confident vs. anxious about sponsorships?"

**Social Jobs:**
- "How do you want sponsors to perceive you?"
- "What impression do you want your reports to make?"
- "How do you want to be seen in the podcasting community?"

#### Pain Points (10 minutes)
- "What's the most frustrating part of managing sponsorships right now?"
- "What keeps you up at night related to sponsorships?"
- "What do you avoid doing because it's too time-consuming?"
- "What problems do you wish someone would solve?"

#### Gains & Aspirations (10 minutes)
- "If you had a magic wand, what would you change about sponsorship management?"
- "What would make you feel like you've 'made it' as a podcaster?"
- "What outcomes would make sponsors want to work with you again?"
- "What would make you feel more professional?"

#### Closing (5 minutes)
- **WTP Probe:** "What would you pay for a tool that solved [top pain point]?"
- **Competitive Context:** "What tools are you using now? What's missing?"
- **Next Steps:** "Would you be interested in testing an early version?"

## Synthesis Framework

### Post-Interview Analysis

**For Each Interview:**
1. **Transcribe:** Full transcription of interview
2. **Extract Jobs:** List functional, emotional, social jobs
3. **Extract Pains:** List pain points with context
4. **Extract Gains:** List desired outcomes and aspirations
5. **Map to Journeys:** Link insights to specific journey stages
6. **Tag Persona:** Confirm or refine persona classification
7. **Identify Quotes:** Capture powerful quotes for storytelling

**Synthesis Template:**
```
Interview: [Persona] - [Date]
Interviewee: [Name/Role]

Jobs Identified:
- Functional: [list]
- Emotional: [list]
- Social: [list]

Pain Points:
1. [Pain] - [Context] - [Frequency]
2. [Pain] - [Context] - [Frequency]

Gains Desired:
1. [Gain] - [Context] - [Importance]
2. [Gain] - [Context] - [Importance]

Journey Insights:
- [Journey Stage]: [Insight]

WTP Signals:
- [Signal] - [Amount/Context]

Competitive Context:
- Current tools: [list]
- Gaps: [list]

Key Quotes:
- "[Quote]" - [Context]
```

### Cross-Interview Synthesis

**After 10 Interviews Per Persona:**

1. **Jobs Clustering:**
   - Group similar jobs across interviews
   - Identify most common jobs
   - Note unique jobs

2. **Pain Prioritization:**
   - Frequency: How many mentioned it?
   - Intensity: How strongly did they feel it?
   - Impact: How does it affect their work?

3. **Gain Prioritization:**
   - Frequency: How many mentioned it?
   - Aspiration: How important is it?
   - Feasibility: Can we deliver it?

4. **Journey Validation:**
   - Confirm journey stages
   - Identify missing stages
   - Note variations

5. **Hypothesis Validation:**
   - Which hypotheses were validated?
   - Which were invalidated?
   - What new hypotheses emerged?

## Output Deliverables

### 1. Interview Summary Report (Per Persona)
- **Format:** Document with synthesis
- **Contents:** Jobs, pains, gains, journey insights, quotes
- **Update:** After each interview set (10 interviews)

### 2. Jobs-to-Be-Done Map
- **Format:** Visual map showing jobs by category
- **Contents:** Functional, emotional, social jobs with frequency
- **Update:** After each persona set

### 3. Pain Point Prioritization Matrix
- **Format:** Matrix (Frequency × Intensity × Impact)
- **Contents:** Ranked pain points per persona
- **Update:** After each persona set

### 4. Gain Opportunity Map
- **Format:** Visual map showing gains by category
- **Contents:** Desired outcomes with feasibility assessment
- **Update:** After each persona set

### 5. Journey Validation Report
- **Format:** Document comparing documented vs. actual journeys
- **Contents:** Validated stages, missing stages, variations
- **Update:** After each persona set
